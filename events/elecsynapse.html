Elecsynapse: Forum on Electrical Engineering Innovation and Emerging Technologies

Elecsynapse highlighted electrical engineering innovation and emerging technologies. The presentations demonstrated how automation, smart monitoring, renewable energy, data systems, and infrastructure design can solve practical problems in agriculture, education, energy use, and public facilities.

Event Documentation

Official Poster and Program

The official Elecsynapse poster and program document the April 10 colloquium sequence and the research topics presented during the online session.

Main Topics / Projects

  • Automated sugarcane cutter with adaptive length, bud viability sensing, and seed counting
  • Web-based retention monitoring and prediction system
  • SNIP: Smart Energy Plug
  • Dual-axis rack-mounted heliostat mirror solar tracking system
  • Counter-rotating vertical wind turbine integrated into streetlamps
  • Underground cabling design for Tinga-Sampaga Bypass Road

Event Significance

This event showed how electrical engineering can combine hardware, software, sensors, renewable energy, data analytics, and infrastructure planning to create solutions for real problems.

Report-Based Details

Expanded Learning Notes

Automation and machine vision

The automated sugarcane project demonstrated how embedded systems, computer vision, and smart detection can improve agricultural processing by identifying cutting length, bud viability, and seed count more consistently.

Data systems and energy awareness

The retention monitoring system and smart energy plug highlighted the value of data-driven decision-making. They showed that monitoring can help institutions track academic outcomes and help users manage energy consumption more responsibly.

Renewable energy and infrastructure

The heliostat mirror system, vertical wind turbine streetlamp, and underground cabling design emphasized energy harvesting, system optimization, safety, and long-term reliability in public infrastructure.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Machine vision supports agriculture by helping detect quality, defects, and seed count more accurately.
2Smart monitoring improves decision-making in energy use, academic retention tracking, and infrastructure maintenance.
3Renewable energy systems need optimization through tracking, reflection, turbine configuration, and proper system design.
4Infrastructure projects require reliability and safety, especially when integrating SCADA, fault detection, and underground systems.

Personal Reflection: Elecsynapse showed me that electrical engineering is highly interdisciplinary. Many projects combined circuits, programming, sensors, automation, databases, renewable energy, and user-centered design. I learned that an electrical engineer must be willing to work across different technologies.

Application of Learning: I can apply these lessons by becoming more open to software, data analysis, sensors, and automation as part of electrical engineering design. Future projects should not be limited to one discipline when better solutions require integration.
