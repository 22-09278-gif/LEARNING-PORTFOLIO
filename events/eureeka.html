This event contributed to my learning by connecting electrical engineering concepts with real-world applications. It showed how technical knowledge can be used to improve systems, communities, infrastructure, sustainability, and professional practice.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Engineering can improve public service through digital platforms like iBarangay that support requests, tracking, validation, and accessibility.
2Automation can improve recycling by reducing manual labor in sorting, crushing, weighing, and valuation.
3User-centered spaces such as study hubs require proper lighting, outlets, comfort, Wi-Fi, accessibility, and safety.

Personal Reflection: EUREEKA helped me see that engineering innovation is not always purely technical. It can also improve community systems, student learning environments, and everyday public services.

Application of Learning: I can apply this by designing future systems with the user in mind, considering data privacy, ease of use, maintenance, cost, scalability, and social impact.
