EUREEKA: Innovating Ideas to Electrical Engineering Research

EUREEKA presented innovative engineering research and technopreneurship ideas that addressed public service, learning spaces, recycling, energy optimization, automation, and renewable power generation.

This event was significant because it demonstrated that electrical engineering innovation can support communities, students, local government, recycling operations, and renewable energy applications.

Report-Based Details

Expanded Learning Notes

iBarangay and digital public service

The iBarangay presentation proposed a Messenger-based and web-based platform for barangay document requests. Its features included real-time tracking, automated validation, QR code verification, flexible document release options, and data privacy considerations.

Automated bottle processing

The bottle processing project addressed small junkshop operations by combining crushing, sorting, weighing, and valuation. It used components such as load cells, programmable controls, and durable mechanical design to improve recycling efficiency.

Focus Point StudyHub and user-centered spaces

The StudyHub project highlighted how electrical layouts, lighting, outlets, Wi-Fi, comfort, and location can support academic productivity. It showed that engineering can improve everyday learning environments.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Engineering can improve public service through digital platforms that support requests, tracking, validation, and accessibility.
2Automation improves recycling by reducing manual labor in sorting, crushing, weighing, and valuation.
3User-centered spaces matter because study hubs require lighting, outlets, comfort, Wi-Fi, accessibility, and safety.
4Engineering innovation has social value when it addresses community problems and improves daily life.

Personal Reflection: EUREEKA helped me see that engineering innovation is not always purely technical. It can also improve community systems, student learning environments, recycling practices, and public services. I learned that engineering becomes more meaningful when it is accessible and useful to people.

Application of Learning: I can apply this by designing future systems with the user in mind, considering data privacy, ease of use, maintenance, cost, scalability, and social impact.
