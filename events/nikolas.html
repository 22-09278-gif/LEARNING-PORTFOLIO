NIKOLAS: Next-Generation Innovators Electrical Engineering Systems

NIKOLAS featured next-generation electrical engineering systems that applied automation, solar energy, smart education, public infrastructure, disaster resilience, and energy management to real-world problems.

Event Documentation

Official Poster and Program

The official NIKOLAS poster and program schedule document the May 8 colloquium and the sequence of next-generation electrical engineering system presentations.

Main Topics / Projects

  • SolarDry Retract: solar-powered automated retractable clothesline
  • Automated dual-output packaging machine for black pepper production
  • Gamified simulation for pneumatics and electropneumatics education
  • Mobile solar-powered system for energy and water resilience
  • Solar concrete walkway at the Barangay Hall of Mabini, Batangas
  • Electrical system assessment and energy management proposal for Rosario East Central School
  • Proposed electrical system design for Enseñando Lipeño

Event Significance

This event showed how electrical engineering can address household convenience, agricultural processing, technical education, disaster response, public lighting, and institutional energy efficiency.

Report-Based Details

Expanded Learning Notes

SolarDry Retract and household automation

SolarDry Retract used rain sensors, sunlight sensors, a motorized retracting mechanism, solar power, and battery backup to protect clothes during rain or humid weather while reducing manual effort and electricity use.

Solar walkway and public infrastructure

The solar concrete walkway integrated photovoltaic panels, tempered glass protection, a charge controller, battery storage, and LED lighting to convert pedestrian infrastructure into a renewable energy source for public spaces.

Energy-water resilience and school energy management

The mobile solar-powered system connected solar PV, battery storage, and water purification for disaster-prone areas. The school proposal highlighted on-grid solar PV, Building Automation Systems, safety assessment, and energy management for public institutions.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Solar-powered automation can solve household and community problems while reducing energy consumption.
2Disaster resilience requires integrated systems such as solar PV, battery storage, and water purification.
3Public infrastructure can generate energy when walkways and lighting systems are designed creatively.
4Energy management in schools improves safety and efficiency through assessment, automation, and renewable energy integration.

Personal Reflection: NIKOLAS taught me that real engineering design must consider actual conditions such as weather, durability, household space, public use, maintenance, and community needs. It made me realize that a design is only successful if it works reliably in the environment where it will be used.

Application of Learning: I can apply this by evaluating future designs based on environmental conditions, user needs, safety, energy storage, accessibility, maintenance, and long-term reliability.
