FrontiEErs 2.0: Foraging the Forefront of Electrical Engineering

FrontiEErs 2.0 focused on practical electrical engineering analysis, particularly voltage drop calculation, mixed-use high-rise building electrical design, and Energy Management Systems. The event connected classroom design concepts with industry practice and facilities management.

Official Poster and Program

The official FrontiEErs 2.0 visual presents the event theme, Zoom setting, and the program flow for voltage drop, building electrical design, and Energy Management Systems.

Main Topics / Projects

  • Voltage drop calculation for low, medium, and high-voltage settings
  • Electrical design analysis for mixed-use high-rise buildings
  • Schematic design phase and basement utility room planning
  • Introduction to Energy Management Systems
  • Facilities planning, operation management, and energy efficiency
  • Industry insights from Engr. Christopher “Perry” T. Bellaflor, PEE, ACPE, ACEM

Event Significance

This seminar strengthened my understanding of real electrical design work. It showed that professional design requires accurate calculations, code-based judgment, proper utility planning, and energy management strategies that support long-term facility performance.

Report-Based Details

Expanded Learning Notes

Voltage drop and design accuracy

The voltage drop discussion showed that electrical calculations directly affect system safety, voltage quality, equipment performance, and user reliability. It reminded me that small design errors can create large operational problems.

High-rise building electrical planning

The building design session emphasized the importance of utility rooms, risers, electrical rooms, layout coordination, and proper planning during the schematic design phase.

Energy management systems

The EMS discussion highlighted how monitoring, evaluation, and efficient operation can reduce waste and improve facility performance. It presented energy management as a professional responsibility, not just a cost-saving tool.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Voltage drop affects safety and performance, so accurate calculations are necessary in electrical design.
2Building electrical design requires coordination among utility rooms, equipment spaces, risers, layouts, and building functions.
3Energy Management Systems help reduce waste by monitoring and improving how facilities use electricity.
4Industry practice requires judgment, not only formulas, because every building has real constraints and operational needs.

Personal Reflection: I realized that electrical design decisions have long-term consequences. A good design must be safe, efficient, maintainable, and responsive to the real needs of the facility. Seeing actual schematic design materials helped me connect the theory of electrical design with professional practice.

Application of Learning: I can apply this in future design tasks by considering voltage drop, correct equipment sizing, electrical room layout, future expansion, energy efficiency, and smart controls from the start of the design process.
