FrontiEErs 2.0: Foraging the Forefront of Electrical Engineering

FrontiEErs 2.0 focused on practical electrical engineering analysis, particularly voltage drop calculation, building electrical design, and Energy Management Systems. The event connected theoretical design principles with actual facility needs.

Main Topics / Projects

  • Voltage drop calculation for low, medium, and high voltage settings
  • Electrical design analysis for mixed-use high-rise buildings

Event Significance

This event contributed to my learning by connecting electrical engineering concepts with real-world applications. It showed how technical knowledge can be used to improve systems, communities, infrastructure, sustainability, and professional practice.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Voltage drop affects safety and performance, so accurate calculations are necessary in electrical design.
2Electrical design must consider actual loads, code compliance, future expansion, cost, and reliability.
3Energy Management Systems help facilities monitor consumption, detect waste, and improve efficiency.

Personal Reflection: I realized that electrical design decisions have long-term consequences. A good design must be safe, efficient, maintainable, and responsive to the real needs of the building or facility.

Application of Learning: I can apply this in future design tasks by considering voltage drop, correct equipment sizing, energy efficiency, and smart controls from the start of the design process.
