ENGrained: Foundational and Emerging Research Directions in Engineering

ENGrained presented research topics that connected foundational engineering concepts with emerging technologies. The event showed how engineering research can address problems in electrical distribution, automation, agriculture, transportation, infrastructure, and engineering education.

Main Topics / Projects

  • Underground electrical distribution and cabling system design
  • Automation in electrical systems and illumination design
  • Automated cornhusk fiber extractor and cordage making
  • Ultra-fast charging system for electric bicycle and tricycle batteries
  • Smart and sustainable electrical infrastructure design
  • Schneider PLC laboratory activity for automated water supply systems

Event Significance

This event contributed to my learning by connecting electrical engineering concepts with real-world applications. It showed how technical knowledge can be used to improve systems, communities, infrastructure, sustainability, and professional practice.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Research begins with real needs such as agricultural waste, energy demand, electrical safety, and limited hands-on learning opportunities.
2Fast charging must balance charging speed with battery temperature, degradation, efficiency, and safety.
3PLC-based training systems can bridge the gap between theoretical learning and actual industrial automation practice.

Personal Reflection: ENGrained helped me appreciate that engineering research can improve farms, laboratories, schools, transportation systems, and local communities through practical and testable solutions.

Application of Learning: I can apply this by approaching future projects through proper problem identification, prototype design, testing, safety considerations, and analysis of cost and usability.
