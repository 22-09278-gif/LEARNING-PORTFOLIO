ENGrained: Foundational and Emerging Research Directions in Engineering

ENGrained presented research topics that connected foundational engineering concepts with emerging technologies. The colloquium showed how engineering research can address problems in electrical distribution, automation, agriculture, transportation, infrastructure, and engineering education.

Official Poster and Program

Main Topics / Projects

  • Underground electrical distribution and cabling system design
  • Automation in electrical and illumination systems
  • Automated cornhusk fiber extraction and cordage making
  • Ultra-fast charging systems for electric bicycle and tricycle batteries
  • Smart and sustainable electrical infrastructure design
  • Schneider PLC laboratory activity for automated water supply systems

Event Significance

This event demonstrated that engineering research can be used to make learning more practical, improve energy systems, and solve problems in agriculture, infrastructure, and transportation.

Report-Based Details

Expanded Learning Notes

Fast charging and battery optimization

The fast-charging study showed that speed should not be the only goal in charging electric vehicle batteries. Charging time, temperature rise, C-rate, state of charge, efficiency, and battery degradation must be balanced to protect battery health.

PLC-based laboratory learning

The Schneider PLC project emphasized the educational value of a modular training system where students can practice tank level monitoring, pump sequencing, valve control, fault simulation, wiring, programming, and troubleshooting.

Research as practical problem solving

The event connected research with actual needs such as sustainable materials, improved automation, and better engineering education. It showed that research becomes meaningful when it can be tested, explained, and applied.

Knowledge Gained

Key Takeaways

1Engineering research starts from real needs, such as agricultural waste, energy demand, transportation electrification, and limited hands-on laboratory experience.
2Fast charging must be optimized to balance charging speed, safety, thermal behavior, and battery lifespan.
3PLC training systems strengthen practical competence by helping students connect theory with industrial automation practice.
4Emerging research must be testable and useful, especially when it is intended for students, communities, or industries.

Personal Reflection: ENGrained helped me appreciate that engineering research can improve farms, laboratories, schools, transportation systems, and local communities through practical and testable solutions. It encouraged me to think of research not just as a requirement, but as a process of designing something useful.

Application of Learning: I can apply this by approaching future projects through proper problem identification, prototype design, testing, safety considerations, and analysis of cost, usability, and long-term value.
