Personal Introduction

My Academic Identity

I am Mark Ramirez, a fourth-year Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering student majoring in Machine Automation and Process Control. This learning portfolio represents my engagement with the seminars and colloquia attended for EE 417, where I explored technical knowledge, research innovation, sustainability, automation, and professional development.

As a student specializing in machine automation and process control, I am particularly interested in systems that combine electrical design, sensors, control logic, automation, and real-world problem solving. The seminars and colloquia helped me see how these areas are applied in power systems, renewable energy, smart infrastructure, automated machines, and community-based engineering solutions.

Through this portfolio, I aim to show not only what I learned from each event, but also how these experiences shaped my understanding of electrical engineering as a profession that requires technical competence, ethical responsibility, creativity, communication, and service to society.
