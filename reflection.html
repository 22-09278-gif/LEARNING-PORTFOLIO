Professional Growth Reflection

This reflection summarizes how the seminars and colloquia shaped my mindset as a future electrical engineer.

Before attending these seminars and colloquia, I viewed electrical engineering mainly as a field focused on computations, circuits, machines, and electrical systems. After completing the EE 417 learning activities, I realized that the discipline is much broader. It requires technical knowledge, creativity, responsibility, communication, ethical awareness, and concern for people and the environment.


One of the strongest lessons I gained is that engineering must be designed with purpose. Every project should respond to a real problem. Whether it involves energy monitoring, solar-powered infrastructure, digital public service, power factor correction, water resilience, or automation, the value of engineering lies in its ability to improve lives and support sustainable development.


I also learned that a good engineering solution must be balanced. It must be functional, but it should also be safe, affordable, maintainable, user-friendly, and appropriate for its environment. The Q&A sessions were useful because they tested each project not only technically, but also practically through questions about cost, reliability, maintenance, accessibility, safety, and long-term use.


Moving forward, I want to become an electrical engineer who is technically competent and socially responsible. I want to continue developing my skills in design, analysis, automation, renewable energy, and communication so I can contribute to solutions that are useful, sustainable, and meaningful.

Growth in Knowledge

I gained exposure to practical electrical engineering areas such as SCADA, EMS, PLC, renewable energy, voltage drop, power quality, automation, and energy optimization.

Growth in Skills

I developed stronger technical listening, reflection writing, research appreciation, and the ability to connect theoretical lessons with real engineering applications.

Growth in Values

I developed greater appreciation for safety, sustainability, ethics, resilience, service, teamwork, and responsibility as important parts of engineering practice.
